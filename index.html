About the Project

Revolutionizing urban traffic management with AI

Abstract

Rapid urbanization and increasing vehicle density have made effective traffic monitoring a critical challenge for modern cities. This project presents an AI-based Road Traffic Analysis System that performs real-time vehicle detection, tracking, and traffic flow analysis using YOLO-V4 and Deep SORT algorithms.

The system processes uploaded traffic videos through a Tkinter-based graphical user interface, where YOLO-V4 is employed for high-speed and accurate vehicle detection, and Deep SORT is used to assign unique IDs and track vehicles across consecutive frames.

Existing Problem

Traditional traffic monitoring systems rely on manual surveillance, static CCTV observation, and sensor-based approaches such as inductive loop detectors. These methods are labor-intensive, error-prone, and lack real-time adaptability.

Manual monitoring cannot efficiently handle high density traffic scenarios, while sensor-based systems are costly to install and maintain. Additionally, conventional rule-based tracking techniques struggle with occlusions, overlapping vehicles, and varying lighting conditions.

Our Proposed Method

The proposed system integrates YOLO-V4 for real-time vehicle detection and Deep SORT for robust multi-object tracking. Users upload traffic videos through a Tkinter-based desktop interface.

Video frames are extracted and resized before being processed by YOLO-V4 to detect vehicles with bounding boxes. Deep SORT then assigns unique IDs to each detected vehicle and tracks their movement across frames using Kalman filtering and cosine distance metrics.

The system displays real-time results including bounding boxes, tracking IDs, vehicle counts, FPS, accuracy, and precision. This automated pipeline ensures accurate, scalable, and real-time traffic analysis suitable for highways, intersections, and urban roads.
